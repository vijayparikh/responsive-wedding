Guest Info

Garba


What is Raas-Garba??

The Raas-Garba is an evening of traditional Indian dance and music. Our Raas-Garba is on Friday night, May 8th. The garba is a traditional Gujurati dance where everyone dances in concentric circles. There are simple garba steps, and there are more complex ones—these involve up to eight steps that are repeated over and over. Usually, the innermost circles have the most complicated steps, and the outer rings are more like people just walking in a circle and clapping. Garba is continuous, however, you don’t have to participate the whole time—people join and leave constantly. The music gradually speeds up and is quite fast and energetic at the end.

The raas music works the same way, but the dance is different—it’s performed with the dandiya, which are pairs of painted footlong wooden sticks. It’s also performed with a partner (same gender, opposite gender, doesn’t matter), and it involves 5 easy steps done over and over again (we’ll teach everyone at the beginning of the night and (you can also look it up on youtube). It is easy to pick up, you can either jump in or learn as you go or practice a few times on the side until you are ready to join in. Raas can be done in pairs, or in a line of couples where people rotate down the line. Sometimes (after 20-30 minutes when you’re ready to get more creative), groups form new lines with more complicated steps; one popular one is often referred to as “12 step”. It’s really, really fun. Raas is also easy come-easy go; the only caveat is that you usually have to join/leave in pairs, so there isn’t an odd person out.


What does one wear to a Raas-Garba?

Raas-Garbas tend to have a carnival atmosphere: lots of kids running around, loud music, and it will probably get hot and sweaty with the dancing. Also, when you get really into Garba and Raas dancing, you end up taking pretty big steps and even jumping around a little or a lot. If you want to buy traditional Indian outfits we will include some websites below and/or you can go to local Indian clothing stores in your area. Men typically wear Kurta/pajamas and women wear “chunia-choli”, or “Ghagra choli” or “lehngha”. If you do not have Indian clothes, men can wear business casual (khakhi/shirt)- no tie needed or Jeans and a nice button down. Women can wear long skirts and a fun top. Think bright colors/beads, sequins, rhinestones are all good. Not too much skin.

Both dances are traditionally done *barefoot*, and bring shoes you don’t mind taking off and leaving in a corner somewhere.
