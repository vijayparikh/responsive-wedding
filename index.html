HOW WE MET

In early 2014, Rupal was busy working in Chicago eating her way through the Zagat restaurant list when our mutual friend asked very nonchalantly if she would like to meet one of her friends. No details were provided. We decided to meet at Webster Wine Bar on March 7th, 2014. This was a quaint little wine bar with great ambiance and food (of course Rupal’s pick). Rupal walked in looking beautiful and glowing, which Asheesh later realized was due to her excitement of a fantastic brand new coat that she was wearing. In either case, we started chatting and Rupal, just like many other people, was interested in picking Asheesh’s brain regarding her hip injury (Asheesh didn’t mind). Anyhow, conversation progressed and so did our relationship. The beginning…

THE PROPOSAL

A few weeks before the proposal, I had told Rupal that I wanted to have a graduation/leaving Chicago celebration with friends. She was helping me pick a venue and the guestlist. The weekend of the proposal, Asheesh’s sister Shivani came into town on Friday. Shivani ensured that they went to get manicures and picked out a dress.Friday night I went to the restaurant and picked out our table near the fireplace and dropped off my camera with the hostess. On Saturday Aug 9th, we had tentatively talked about meeting up at “RM Champagne Room” (one of our first date spots) before the graduation party. I arrived early to make sure everything was arranged according to plan. Meanwhile, Rupal was waiting for me to call her to confirm plans for that evening. When she realized that I was already there she started wondering why I didn’t call her before leaving and also realized that the location and timing didn’t make a whole lot of sense. So she started the process of convincing me that this is not a sound plan and that we should change the plan and meet closer to the party venue. This back and forth took a while but eventually I convinced her to show up. While I waited back at RM Champagne Lounge, the staff was eagerly awaiting Rupal’s arrival (and probably getting a little nervous for me). Rupal finally rushed in, (invisalign still in the mouth) sat down at the table and started laughing, wondering why I was seated alone at a table with no one around sipping Champagne. I told her not to worry about all that and that maybe she should go to the bathroom and get sorted. Once we got past all that, I suggested we have a state of the union address (regarding our relationship). Of course, I had to again convince her to go first. When it was my turn, I got down on one knee and asked to her to marry me. Everyone in the restaurant started clapping, and the camera flashes were going off, making Rupal’s verbal response unclear. However, we are planning a wedding so I assume she said yes. As it turned out, the “graduation” party Rupal was planning was a surprise engagement party for us.
